Consignment Terms

Version 1.0 — Last updated: February 2026

These terms apply to any business venue ("Venue", "you") that stocks Could products ("we", "us") on a consignment basis and accepts these terms by choosing the "I accept" option in our setup/order form (the "Setup Form") or otherwise confirming an order.

1) Definitions and acceptance

  • Stock: Could products delivered to the Venue.
  • Deal Terms: the commercial terms confirmed in the Setup Form (e.g., flavours, quantity, RRP, margin, payment timing).

By accepting these terms, you confirm you're authorised to bind the Venue.

If these terms conflict with the Deal Terms, the Deal Terms win only for the commercial fields.

2) Consignment basics

  • We deliver Stock for the Venue to sell to its customers.
  • Ownership stays with Could until a unit is sold to a customer.
  • The Venue sells Stock as a retailer in its own name.

3) Pricing and selling rules

  • The Venue will sell at the RRP in the Deal Terms and will not discount/bundle Stock in a way that reduces the effective price, unless indicated otherwise in the Setup Form.
  • The Venue keeps the margin in the Deal Terms; Could is owed the balance for units sold.
  • No resale to third parties or online without our written approval.

4) Storage, handling, and dates

  • Stock must be stored chilled and handled with reasonable care, following label instructions.
  • The Venue must not sell Stock after its best-before/expiry date.

5) Loss, damage, and spoilage

From delivery, the Venue is responsible for Stock that is lost, stolen, damaged, or spoiled due to improper storage/handling, except to the extent caused by our defect or delivery issue.

6) Reconciliation (how we count what sold)

Unless we agree otherwise in writing:

  • At each restock/visit we may count remaining Stock on site.
  • Units sold = units delivered since the last count − units remaining.
  • The Venue will provide reasonable access to the Stock for counting during normal opening hours. If access is repeatedly refused, we may pause deliveries until resolved.

7) Invoicing and payment

  • After each reconciliation, we invoice for units sold (net of the Venue margin).
  • Payment timing is per the Deal Terms (default Net 7 days).
  • Late payment may incur statutory interest and recovery costs under the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998.

8) Unsold Stock, rotation, and expiry

  • We may rotate Stock during restocks.
  • We may (at our discretion) collect unsold saleable Stock if consignment ends.
  • Stock is "saleable" only if unopened, undamaged, correctly stored, and within date. We do not credit/buy back Stock that is not saleable.

9) Termination

Either party can end consignment on 7 days' notice by email. We may end immediately for material breach (including non-payment or repeated improper storage/handling).

10) Product issues and recalls

  • We warrant Stock is produced and labelled in line with applicable UK food law.
  • If there's a recall/safety notice, the Venue must stop selling immediately and follow instructions.

11) Liability (simple cap)

Nothing limits liability that cannot legally be limited (e.g., death/personal injury due to negligence, fraud).

Subject to that, Could's total liability is capped at the amount the Venue paid to Could in the 3 months before the event. We are not liable for indirect or consequential losses.

12) General

  • No partnership/agency is created.
  • Governing law and courts: England & Wales.
  • We may update these terms by publishing a new version; new versions apply to Stock delivered after the update date.
